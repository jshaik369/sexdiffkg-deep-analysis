## RARE/COMMON AE SEX GRADIENT

### Gradient Confirmed
| AE Rarity | N Signals | %Female | Effect Size |
|-----------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Ultra-rare (1-3 drugs) | 3,805 | 46.5% | 0.923 |
| Rare (4-10) | 6,597 | 49.9% | 0.939 |
| Uncommon (11-50) | 25,456 | 51.4% | 0.964 |
| Common (51-100) | 19,402 | 52.2% | 0.973 |
| Frequent (101-500) | 40,520 | **57.5%** | 1.022 |

### Correlation: r = +0.047, p = 7.16e-49
More commonly reported AEs have stronger female bias.
Ultra-rare AEs are sex-balanced; frequent AEs are female-biased.

### Interpretation
1. Common AEs (many drugs cause them) have a systematic female excess
2. This could reflect: (a) biological sensitivity, (b) reporting bias, or (c) higher female drug exposure
3. The gradient is statistically significant but small in magnitude (r=0.047)
4. Ultra-rare AEs may be more biologically specific and less subject to reporting artifacts
