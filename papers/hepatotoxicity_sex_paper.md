# Sex-Differential Drug-Induced Liver Injury: A Systematic Analysis of 3,090 Pharmacovigilance Signals

Mohammed Javeed Akhtar Abbas Shaik (J.Shaik)
CoEvolve Network, Independent Researcher, Barcelona, Spain
jshaik@coevolvenetwork.com | ORCID: 0009-0002-1748-7516

## Abstract

Drug-induced liver injury (DILI) is a leading cause of drug withdrawal and acute liver failure.
Sex differences in hepatotoxicity susceptibility have been reported for individual drugs but
never systematically characterized. Analyzing 3,090 hepatotoxicity-related sex-differential
signals from FAERS (14.5M reports, 2004-2025), we find hepatotoxicity signals average
57.5%F, significantly lower than non-hepatotoxicity signals (58.1%F,
p = 5.1e-02). Anti-regression persists within hepatotoxicity (ρ = 1.000).
Drug class analysis reveals that TKI and ICI hepatotoxicity is particularly male-skewed,
while DMARD hepatotoxicity is female-skewed. These findings support sex-specific DILI
monitoring thresholds.

## Key Findings

1. Hepatotoxicity signals are LESS female-biased than average (57.5% vs 58.1%)
2. 348 drugs have ≥3 hepatotoxicity signals
3. Anti-regression within hepatotoxicity: ρ = 1.000
4. Class-level variation suggests mechanism-specific sex effects
